Signature Keynote

The Augmented Human: What's Left for Humans When Machines Outperform Us?

Machines will surpass us in intelligence. Soon. So — what's left for us?

This keynote starts from that uncomfortable question and challenges the way we think about AI, leadership, and human value. Daniela explores a dual approach to AI adoption — top-down and bottom-up — showing that sustainable transformation doesn't start with tools, but with mindset. Because people don't change because they are told to. They change when they have their own reasons to move.

  • A clear realization that people are still the most valuable asset — and that AI won't replace those who have meaning and purpose in what they do.
  • A deeper understanding that leadership is about motivation — giving people real reasons to embrace AI.
  • A shift in perspective: seeing AI adoption not just as a technological challenge, but as a human and strategic one.
  • A practical understanding of the dual adoption model: when to use each approach, and how to make them work together.

About

Daniela Malqui

Daniela Malqui is an international speaker, strategy and operations leader, and founder of Doing Minds — a firm focused on automation and applied AI to optimize processes and drive real execution.

With over 13 years of experience and having lived and worked across five countries, she brings a truly global and grounded perspective shaped by working hands-on with teams, leaders, and organizations navigating change.

Her keynote, The Augmented Human, starts with an uncomfortable but necessary question: what makes us truly human in a world where machines are becoming increasingly capable? Through a powerful and practical lens, Daniela introduces a dual approach to AI adoption — showing that real transformation doesn't start with tools, but with people who have a reason to move.

She has spoken at AWSome Summit, INNOVaiTECH, Devconnect, DCN, Foro Blockchain, ETH México, Labitconf, Talent Land, and more — and has led immersive AI workshops across LATAM, training over 1,000 people in 2025. A hackathon winner and passionate educator, Daniela is recognized for turning complex ideas into experiences that shift mindset, spark action, and stay with people long after the talk ends.

She speaks Spanish, English, and Hebrew, and is known for her ability to connect deeply with diverse audiences.
